Gemstone Basics

Understand what makes a gemstone natural, how quality is judged, and why certification and treatments matter.

What are Natural Gemstones?

Natural gemstones are minerals (and in some cases organic materials) formed inside the Earth over thousands to millions of years. Pressure, temperature and trace elements give them their unique colours and internal patterns.

People wear gemstones for three main reasons:

  • Astrology & Vedic remedies – to strengthen a beneficial planet.
  • Healing & energy work – to balance chakras, emotions and subtle energy.
  • Jewellery & investment – for beauty, status and long-term value.

A single gemstone can fulfil more than one purpose – for example, a Yellow Sapphire ring can be an astrological remedy and also a beautiful piece of jewellery.

Main Categories of Gemstones

Precious Stones

Traditionally considered the most valuable due to rarity, demand and durability.

  • Diamond – brilliance, luxury, Venus.
  • Ruby – royal red, Sun.
  • Blue Sapphire – deep blue, Saturn.
  • Emerald – rich green, Mercury.

Semi-Precious & Healing Stones

Wider range of colours and prices – excellent for healing, crystal grids and everyday jewellery.

  • Amethyst, Citrine, Peridot, Garnet
  • Tourmaline, Aquamarine, Moonstone
  • Black Tourmaline, Lapis Lazuli, Aventurine, etc.

Organic & Special Materials

Formed from living organisms or natural processes, not minerals in the strict sense.

  • Pearl – formed in oysters.
  • Coral – marine skeleton.
  • Amber – fossil tree resin.
  • Rudraksha – sacred seed used like a spiritual “gem”.

Natural vs. Treated vs. Synthetic

Almost all gemstones are cut and polished, but some are also treated to improve colour or clarity. It’s important to understand these terms, especially for astrology and investment.

Category What it means Common uses Astrological effect
Natural (Untreated) Formed in nature, only cut & polished. No colour/clarity treatment. Best choice for remedies, long-term value and serious collectors. Strongest and most stable.
Treated Natural stone that has been heated, oiled or filled to enhance appearance. (e.g. heat-treated blue sapphire, oiled emerald). Acceptable for jewellery and moderate-budget astrology, if treatment is disclosed and stable. Effect is usually medium – still natural, but somewhat modified.
Synthetic / Lab-grown Man-made in a lab; chemically similar to natural stone but not formed in Earth. Great for fashion jewellery where budget and look are priority. No traditional astrological benefit.

Always ask: Is this stone natural? Has it been treated? Is it certified? A good seller will share this clearly.

How Gemstone Quality is Judged

Like diamonds, coloured gemstones are evaluated using the “4 Cs” plus a few extra factors:

1. Colour

The most important factor. For most stones, medium to vivid colour (not too dark, not too pale) is preferred. Uniform colour without dull patches increases value.

2. Clarity

Natural inclusions are normal. Stones with fewer visible cracks, clouds or spots are more valuable. Some stones (like emerald) are almost always included – completely clean pieces are rare.

3. Cut & Shape

A good cut brings out brilliance and even colour. Poor cutting can make a stone look dull or smaller. Popular shapes include oval, cushion, round, pear and emerald cut.

4. Carat Weight

Carat is the weight of the gemstone, not its size. Larger, clean stones of high colour quality rise in price very quickly compared to smaller sizes.

5. Origin & Certification

Certain locations are famous for specific stones – for example, Burmese ruby, Ceylon sapphire, Colombian emerald. Origin can influence price, but correct certification is more important.

For high-value purchases, always insist on a valid gemstone report from a recognised lab.

Understanding Gemstone Certification

A gemstone certificate (lab report) is an independent opinion about the stone’s identity, weight, basic quality and whether it is natural, treated or synthetic.

Trusted Labs

  • IGI – International Gemological Institute
  • GIA – Gemological Institute of America
  • GRS, GII, SGL and other reputed national labs

Key Points to Check

  • Stone name & variety (e.g. Natural Corundum – Ruby).
  • Weight in carats / colour description.
  • “Natural” vs “Synthetic”.
  • Treatments mentioned (e.g. “Heated”, “Oiled”, “No indications of treatment”).

For everyday semi-precious stones, small seller certificates are common. For expensive gemstones, prefer reports from well-known labs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a gemstone is real?

Ask for a certificate from a recognised lab, check that details on the report match the stone, and buy from a trusted seller. Very “cheap” offers on high-value stones are usually a red flag.

Are synthetic stones useful in astrology?

No. In traditional Vedic astrology only natural gemstones are used, because the planetary energy is linked to the natural formation process inside the Earth.

Can I start with a smaller carat weight and upgrade later?

Yes. Even a smaller but good-quality stone can help you begin a remedy or healing journey. You can always upgrade to a higher weight or finer quality in future as budget allows.
