Quick authenticity tips

  • Compare weight/size vs price — unusually cheap often means treated.

Gemstone Buying Guide — What to check

A short checklist to verify authenticity, suitability and the right metal/setting for astrological use.

1. Which gemstone is ideal for you?

Each gemstone corresponds to a ruling planet. Use the free DOB recommendation tool or consult an astrologer to find your suitable gemstones.

2. How to select quality (Cut • Color • Clarity • Carat • Origin)

  • Cut: Well-cut stones give proper brilliance.
  • Color: Natural, balanced color. Avoid overly uniform/dyed stones.
  • Clarity: Normal inclusions okay; bubbles = synthetic.
  • Carat vs Price: Too cheap → usually treated/synthetic.
  • Origin: Provenance adds value (e.g., Burma Ruby, Colombian Emerald).

3. Certification & labs

Always request third-party lab certificates. Trusted labs include GIA, IGI, GRS, Gubelin, SSEF.

4. Metals & mounting — what to choose

Gold, silver, or panchdhatu depending on gemstone and planet.

5. Where to purchase & red flags

  • Always purchase from a gemstone expert with certification.
  • Be cautious if seller refuses lab report or hides treatments.
  • Red flags: Too cheap, no returns, no certificate.

6. Acceptance & preparation

Prefer untreated stones for astrology. Emeralds may be oiled and still acceptable.
